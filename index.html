Homework 1: Reviewing Games from Different Genres

For this assignment we were tasked with finding 3 games and getting positive and negative feedback for each. I chose Rocket League, Dead Cells, and Counter Strike. At the time I was really into Dead Cells which is a pretty intense single player Roguelite 2D platformer dungeon crawler. The other two Are competetive multiplayer games. None of these games are very forgiving and each comes with a good amount of challenges to progress.

I think that I like all of these games because they are very challenging and very rewarding. I think that this is usually my favorite part of a game is overcoming challenges and the feeling of pride which follows. One of my other favorite games which didn't make the cut was Dark Souls 3 which is a very similar game to Dead Cells in it's difficulty and relative frequency of bossfights. The difficulty seems to be the prime draw in all of these games, with most of the difficulty in Counter Strike coming from attempting to cooperate with your self destructive teammates. Overall super satisfying games to play when you finally overcome those challenges and win.

Homework 2: What makes up a game?

For this assignment we were tasked with analyzing and suggesting improvements for a simple pen and paper game and comparing two dissimilar games. For my games I picked a rail tycoon boardgame called Ticket to Ride alongside Call of Duty Warzone. I thought these would be fun to compare because they are wildly different in gameplay but they are rather similar in the progression of the game. I made the statement that both games are driven by positioning and economy moreso than skill. Warzone is still highly skill dependent but a worse team can still beat a better team with better consumables (bought with money), tactics, and position. In Ticket to Ride a good established main rail in a highly populated area can spell a late game snowball where the player no longer has to place roads and can just acquire already route cards which they have almost completed already given their position on the board.

For the game analysis section I wasn't exactly sure on the rules of the game so I proposed multiple rulechanges for each possible gamerules. That game could only be so fun though so there was not a huge room for improvement.
